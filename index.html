Welcome to ISKCON Devdaha

ISKCON Devdaha stands as a sacred spiritual center where devotees and seekers from all walks of life gather to immerse themselves in the teachings of Lord Krishna. Surrounded by divine peace and filled with the sound of kirtans, the temple inspires every visitor to connect with their inner self. Here, one can experience the essence of Bhakti Yoga, the joy of devotional service, and the happiness that comes from chanting the holy names. Our mission is to share the timeless wisdom of the Bhagavad Gita, guide individuals toward spiritual enlightenment, and create a global community rooted in love, compassion, and devotion. Whether you are beginning your spiritual journey or deepening your existing practice, ISKCON Devdaha welcomes you with open arms and a pure heart.

About Srila Prabhupada

Srila A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada, the founder-acharya of ISKCON, is one of the greatest spiritual ambassadors of Sanatana Dharma in modern history. With unshakable faith in the order of his spiritual master, he journeyed alone to the West at the age of 69, carrying only a trunk of books and the message of Lord Krishna. Through his purity, determination, and divine compassion, he established a global movement that touched millions of hearts. His teachings continue to guide devotees in understanding the true purpose of life, the path of Bhakti Yoga, and the eternal relationship between the soul and Krishna.

Srila Prabhupada translated and wrote elaborate purports on sacred scriptures such as the Bhagavad Gita As It Is, Srimad Bhagavatam, Chaitanya Charitamrita, and many more timeless works. His books act as a spiritual lighthouse, illuminating the path for seekers who wish to rise above material confusion and taste the bliss of Krishna Consciousness. His legacy lives on in every ISKCON temple, every kirtan, every devotee, and every soul who comes closer to God through his teachings.

Timeline of Srila Prabhupada

  • 1896: Birth of Abhay Charan De in Kolkata.
  • 1933: Meets his spiritual master Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati Thakura.
  • 1965: Travels to the USA on the Jaladuta.
  • 1966: Establishes ISKCON in New York.
  • 1970–77: Opens 108 temples worldwide & publishes many spiritual books.

Major Achievements

  • Established the worldwide Hare Krishna Movement.
  • Translated over 80 volumes of spiritual literature.
  • Opened more than 108 ISKCON temples in 11 years.
  • Inspired millions globally to take up Bhakti Yoga.
  • Started prasadam distribution programs worldwide.

Govardhana Hill — Krishna's Protection

As a child in Vrindavan, Krishna lifted the Govardhana Hill on his little finger to shelter the villagers from torrential rains sent by Indra, the king of the heavens. The villagers and their cattle stood safely beneath the hill for seven days and nights while Krishna held it aloft. This act showed that by devotion and humility the Lord protects those who surrender to Him. The Govardhana episode teaches practical compassion, community care, and the supremacy of loving service over pride and ritual.
